## Structure

Narrative Scientific Structure

Used in review papers or interdisciplinary journals.

- Title
- Abstract
- Introduction
- Background
- Analysis / Findings
- Discussion
- Conclusion
- References

## Typical Structure of a Scientific Journal Article (IMRaD Model)

- <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IMRAD>

1. **Title**
   Concise statement of the research topic.

2. **Author Information**
   - Author names
   - Institutional affiliations
   - Corresponding author contact

3. **Abstract**
   Short summary containing:
   - research objective
   - methods
   - key results
   - main conclusion

4. **Keywords**
   3–6 indexing terms describing the topic.

5. **Introduction**
   - Background and context
   - Review of relevant literature
   - Research gap
   - Research objective or hypothesis

6. **Methods (Methodology)**
   - Study design
   - Data sources / participants / materials
   - Procedures and data collection
   - Analytical or statistical methods

7. **Results**
   - Presentation of empirical findings
   - Tables, figures, statistical outputs
   - No interpretation

8. **Discussion**
   - Interpretation of results
   - Relation to existing research
   - Theoretical or practical implications
   - Study limitations

9. **Conclusion**
   - Direct answer to research question
   - Key takeaway of the study
   - Possible future research directions

10. **References**
    Full bibliographic list of cited works.

11. **Appendix / Supplementary Material (optional)**

- extended tables
- questionnaires
- additional analyses
- datasets or technical details.
